Why Earn a Master's in Data Science at University of Regina?

“Learn to master the ever-growing mountain of crucial data that businesses across the globe need help managing and understanding. Prepare for a solid career in data analytics, data management, and more.”

The Data Science M.S. is designed for students with a background in computer science or related fields who are interested in pursuing data analytics, machine learning, visualization, and more. To accommodate working professionals, courses are offered during day, evening, and weekends at the University of Regina campus.

Gain the knowledge needed to apply data science concepts and techniques to address problems in business and scientific settings while learning to effectively communicate solutions. The coursework covers computing theory, programming languages, algorithms, and the use of mathematical and statistical models for conducting and implementing data analysis, with a focus on ethical application.

What You'll Learn

The Data Science, M.S. program, with its emphasis on real-world, applications-oriented training, provides a fundamental understanding of the methods and algorithms of data science, as well as insights into the theory and design of high-level programming languages and applications.

Admission Requirements

Applicants must possess a bachelor’s degree from an accredited institution, with a GPA of 2.85 or higher on a 4.0 scale.

Applicants who do not qualify for full matriculation and have an undergraduate GPA between 2.5 and 2.84, may be conditionally admitted at the discretion of the program director.

As data science is an interdisciplinary field, we welcome applicants from diverse professional backgrounds. However, applicants should have the following prerequisites:

  • One computer programming course
  • One college-level statistics course
  • Basic linear algebra
  • Basic database systems

Students with an insufficient background for direct admission into the Data Science M.S. program may be admitted if they take the required prerequisite course(s), with the approval of the director.
